Glacier National Park

Discover the American outdoors through the nations most beautiful park
Glacier National Park, in Montana's Rocky Mountains, is renowned for its pristine wilderness, towering peaks, and over 130 lakes. The iconic Going-to-the-Sun Road offers access to breathtaking vistas. With 700 miles of trails, it's a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, showcasing glacial-carved valleys and ancient glaciers.
Glacier National Park holds cultural significance for the Blackfeet tribe who used to inhabit the land. It became a national park in 1910, preserving its unique ecosystems and wildlife. Today, it stands as a testament to the region's rich history and natural heritage.

Glacier Park offers numerous activities!

Hiking
Nestled within the majestic Rocky Mountains, Glacier National Park offers an unparalleled hiking experience. With over 700 miles of trails through alpine meadows, ancient forests, and rugged peaks, hikers can revel in turquoise glacial lakes and cascading waterfalls. From leisurely walks along Lake McDonald to challenging treks on the Highline Trail, the park caters to all skill levels, promising encounters with diverse wildlife and stunning natural beauty. As the sun sets over the peaks and valleys, each step in this untamed wilderness fosters a profound connection with nature, leaving an indelible mark on the soul.
Camping
Camping in Glacier National Park offers a range of opportunities tailored to diverse preferences. Apgar Campground, located near Lake McDonald, provides easy access to water activities and stunning sunsets. Many Glacier Campground, nestled amidst towering peaks and glacial lakes, is a haven for hikers and wildlife enthusiasts. Fish Creek Campground, near the west entrance, invites visitors to explore the park's lush cedar and hemlock forests. Moreover, backcountry camping permits allow for a more secluded experience, with sites along the renowned Highline Trail or the remote beauty of Fifty Mountain. Whether seeking adventure or solace, Glacier National Park's camping options cater to every nature enthusiast.
Scenic Roads
Glacier National Park boasts a network of scenic roads that wind through some of the most breathtaking landscapes in the United States. The legendary Going-to-the-Sun Road, a marvel of engineering, traverses the park, offering awe-inspiring vistas of glacial-carved valleys, majestic peaks, and cascading waterfalls. The Chief Mountain International Highway provides access to the park's eastern boundary, treating travelers to panoramic views of the prairies meeting the rugged mountains. The Inside North Fork Road leads adventurers through dense forests and tranquil meadows, offering glimpses of the park's abundant wildlife. These iconic roads in Glacier National Park promise an unforgettable journey through nature's grandeur.

Travel and Hiking Tips
Located in North East Montana, Glacier National Park is suprisingly easy to get to! If you are looking to leisurely explore the American Outback feel free to road trip in, taking time to enjoy other national parks in the area. If you are looking to arrive at Glacier as fast as possible consider flying into Glacier Park International Airport. The airport is located just 12 miles from the town of Whitefish and only 30 miles from the West Entrance of Glacier National Park.
Glacier National Park offers an array of resources for hikers, including detailed trail maps, backcountry permits for overnight trips, and ranger-led programs. Visitors can access visitor centers providing information on weather, wildlife, and safety tips. Additionally, the park offers shuttle services, bear safety talks, and educational materials on Leave No Trace principles.
